About

Annette Opas

Born and raised in Halifax, Nova Scotia, I've always felt most at home near the ocean. The rhythm of the waves, the salty air, and the grounding presence of nature continue to inspire the way I move, teach, and live.

My yoga journey began as a personal search for something deeper — a way to quiet the noise, soften the pressures of everyday life, and reconnect with myself. What I found was far more than a physical practice. Yoga became a path home.

Over the past nine years, yoga has supported me through life's highs and lows, teaching me how to slow down, listen inward, and trust myself again. It has reminded me that strength and softness can exist together, and that healing often begins in the moments when we allow ourselves to simply be.

Today, I have the privilege of sharing this practice through group classes, private sessions, workplace wellness programs, and online offerings. Whether you're stepping onto the mat for the very first time or deepening an existing practice, my intention is always the same: to create a welcoming space where you can arrive exactly as you are, without judgment.

My classes are rooted in breath, mindfulness, and self-compassion. Together, we might build heat through movement, settle into stillness, or simply take a few conscious breaths. Whatever the practice holds, my hope is that you leave feeling a little more grounded, a little more connected, and a little more at home within yourself.

When I'm not teaching, you'll likely find me walking by the ocean, hiking local trails, travelling, spending time with family, listening to music, reading a good book, or cuddling up with a furry friend.

Thank you for being here.

June 2026

Summer Solstice

The Summer Solstice arrives each year as a gentle reminder to pause and notice. It marks the longest day of the year — a celebration of light, growth, and all that has been unfolding beneath the surface, often without us realizing it.

Nature doesn't rush into bloom. It unfolds in its own time. And perhaps we're not so different.

As I reflect on this season, I'm reminded that growth doesn't always look dramatic. Sometimes it looks like setting a boundary, taking a deep breath before reacting, resting when we need rest, and showing up for ourselves in small, consistent ways.

This year, the solstice feels especially meaningful. It arrives as I'm creating this new space and finding fresh ways to share the practice that has supported me for so many years.

If you've been practicing with me lately, you've probably heard me speak about coming home to yourself. The Summer Solstice feels like an invitation to do exactly that — to step into the light, acknowledge how far you've come, and trust the season you're in.

As we move into the fullness of summer, my hope is that you find moments to slow down, soak in the warmth, and reconnect with what truly matters.

Be well,
Annette
